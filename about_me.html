About Myself

Hello, my name is Lucas, I’m 22 years old finance student with a once strong passion for strength training and muscle building.

One thing I want to clarify right off the bat is that I’m not a certified personal trainer; I don’t hold any official certifications & qualifications in the fitness realm, nor have I trained actual people as clients, however I gave numerous people lifting & nutrition advice, as well as trained some under my supervision (again, not as official clients), which they are thankful for to this day. This site is something I created based off years of personal experience in the fitness field which led me to wanting to share my knowledge and help other individuals achieve their fitness goals and lead healthier lives.

I joined the gym at 16 years of age, right before I turned 17. I grew up as a skinny kid weighing at around 52-53 kilograms while being 173 cm. Can’t say I was too bothered by it at the time nor was I bullied or picked up by classmates, but I certainly wasn’t too happy about my looks either. Nonetheless I decided to join the gym to finally gain some muscle and weight. At the time I didn’t have any knowledge or expertise on proper training, recovery, and nutrition whatsoever. I just went 3-4 times a week, threw in random exercises (mostly bicep curls and bench press), went home, and repeat it all next week.

After couple of months, I started noticing changes which made me even more motivated. After nearly 8 months of training with slightly modified, but more or less same training routine I barely noticed any significant changes – my progress stagnated. I then started to do heavy research on optimal ways of training, eating, and recovering by watching videos and reading articles. Gradually I incorporated it in my training and noticed significant changes overtime. Of course, it’d be impossible for me to apply all that knowledge I gradually gained overnight; muscle building is a slow process which also requires experience.

As a result of research, hard work and decent amount of experience my progress skyrocketed, I gained around 1,3 kg of bodyweight a month (while being on a high caloric surplus). I reached my heaviest weight in March of 2023, at 21 years of age – 68 kgs. But then, unfortunately due to some health problems I had to abandon my “hobby”. Since then I lost about 10 kilos as well as most of my strength and muscle, but knowledge and experienced stayed. So, if you are a growing skinny teenager, you can probably, or at least partly relate to this, but even if aren’t, I hope this page can give you a better understanding on who I am and help you in achieving your fitness goals.
