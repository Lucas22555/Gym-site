Nutrition and diet are crucial components in achieving optimal muscle growth, strength gains, and overall performance. This is the key to getting big/lean. Even if you train hard in the gym and get proper rest but neglect your diet, you won’t see sufficient desired results, mediocre at best. The key to your diet is finding the right balance in macronutrients like protein, carbohydrates, and fats.

Protein

Protein is the most important micronutrient for bodybuilders and is essential for muscle repair and growth. Aim to consume around 1.2 to 2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day. Good sources include lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy products, and plant-based sources like tofu, tempeh, and legumes.

Carbohydrates

Carbs provide the body with glucose, which is converted to energy used to support bodily functions and physical activity. They’re the fuel of the body. Good sources of carbs include bread, beans, pasta, milk, potatoes, fruits especially bananas.

Fats

Healthy fats are important for hormone regulation, help our body to absorb vital nutrients, provide needed energy in form of calories and overall health. Sources for are nuts, peanut butter seeds, avocados, olive oil, and meats and fish

Bulking and cutting

Caloric Surplus or Deficit: To build muscle, you typically need to consume more calories than you burn (caloric surplus), whereas to lose fat, you need to consume fewer calories than you burn (caloric deficit).

Bulking: During a bulking phase, focus on eating slightly above your maintenance calories to support muscle growth. This typically involves increasing your intake of all three macronutrients. This usually involves eating around 200-500 calories above maintenance levels. Can be more depending on how fast you wish to gain weight. It isn’t recommended to eat more than that since then you’ll gain more fat rather than muscle.

Cutting: During a cutting phase, prioritize protein intake to preserve muscle mass while reducing overall calorie intake. This often involves decreasing carbohydrates and fats while maintaining or slightly increasing protein intake. Usually involves eating around 200-500 calories below maintenance. Here it isn’t recommended to eat below 500 calorie mark since it’s starts to get harder both mentally and physically (you practically starve yourself), which will obviously affect your gains.

Summary

  • Strictly follow your diet with emphasis on getting your daily protein – about 1.2 grams protein per kg of bodyweight,
  • Whether bulking or cutting, try maintaining 300-500 caloric deficit or surplus.
  • Do meal preps.
  • Stay hydrated.
